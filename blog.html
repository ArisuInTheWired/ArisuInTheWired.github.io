Entry 01: why science is broken but still the best system to gather knowledge

The moderne scientific method is based on 2 flawed assumptions: the past predicts the future, and the there is a material world behind the qualia we have.

The first assumption is quite simple to understand: every protocol, studies made using the current sientific methodology uses past data to predict future effects and extract patterns. But there are no reasons in the universe why that should be true. The issue is worse than it seems when you think about it. We can't prove or disprove our assumption. Without recursively making the assumption that the past predicts the future.

The second assumption is more philosophical. The scientific method has as it premise the fact that our observation comes from a real, material world. But it isn't a trivial thing. Kant, Descartes and many other philosophers have discussed this very question (does anithing exist beside my qualias) but no one has a real answer. This is a frustrationg realisation, but we can't prove that their is a real world behind our own senses.

Why that isn't a big deal? If we want to predict or understand anything, those two assumptions are a requirement. Also, any methodology will have some assumptions that can't be proved or disproved; this is also the case in more formal fields like mathematics or logic. But studiing the hidden assumptions behind the scientific method still interesting and can open our eyes on other assumptions we make everywhere, even in our daily lives.
